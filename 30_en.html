Chapter 30. Answers and Decisions

The warm hush that had settled over the shelter didn’t last.

Lis was the one who broke it.

There was something sharp in the way she moved — like she’d just shrugged off a cloak of reflection that had grown too heavy.

“Alright. Enough philosophy.”

She turned to Max, arms crossed, and her eyes pinned him where he sat — calm, unblinking, merciless.

“So tell me: why did you scale forty-three stories of sheer concrete just to crash into a corporate data center? Were you that desperate to see me again?”

Max exhaled, running a hand through his hair, the ghost of adrenaline still clinging to his skin. The weight of the past few hours bore down on his shoulders.

“I was trying to get inside… figure out who ordered the professor’s abduction,” he said. “But then I ran into you. And, well — things went a little sideways.”

His voice trailed off. His gaze drifted.

The file he’d stolen — it was the only thing he had left. The final thread still tying him to the truth.

“I’ve got a video,” he said quietly. “Security feed. The person who gave the order — they’re on it. I don’t know who they are… but maybe you will.”

Lis’s expression didn’t change, but something flickered in her eyes — hope, too brittle to hold. Pain, raw and barely hidden.

“This could change everything,” she said. “If we find my father… He knew the corporation inside and out. He could’ve helped.”

Her voice caught — just a hitch — before she shut it down again. Lips pressed into a line sharp enough to cut.

Lawrence watched her in silence, then turned to Max, his gaze weighing unspoken calculations.

“You think they’re holding them together?” he asked her. His voice was low, edged with something close to dread.

“It’s possible,” Lis answered. “We don’t know enough.”

Lawrence sighed and dragged a hand down his face, as if trying to wipe away the exhaustion etched into it.

“Then we start tomorrow. First thing.” He looked around the room. His tone was firm now, decisive. “Tonight — we rest. Tomorrow, we work.”

He turned to Max.

“You staying? We’ve got some rooms.”

Max hesitated. His mind still hovered in survival mode — weighing exits, calculating risk. But deep down, something else made the decision for him.

“Yeah,” he said. “I think I should. After everything that’s happened… I doubt the corporation isn’t already hunting for me.”

“Smart call.” Lawrence clapped a hand on his shoulder — part welcome, part contract.

But Lis didn’t move. Her eyes were fixed now on a different door — the one behind which Green had been locked away. Her stance had shifted. Still and silent, she radiated a dangerous kind of focus.

“Not until we question the traitor.”

The lights overhead gave a faint sizzle. Somewhere deep in the bunker, the generator throbbed like a mechanical heartbeat.

She turned and strode across the room, flinging open the door. The space beyond was dim, stale, unwelcoming. The kind of silence that keeps its secrets.

Lis stepped inside. Max followed, slower.

Royce planted himself in the doorway, arms crossed. He said nothing — didn’t need to. His eyes tracked everything.

In the corner, slumped against the wall, sat Green.

The expensive suit he’d worn like armor was stained now, disheveled. His face was pale. His eyes… empty. Whatever defiance he’d carried was long gone. What remained was a man waiting for judgment.

“I know I’m done,” he rasped. His voice was brittle, cracking like paper. “The corporation doesn’t forgive failure. They’ll erase me. If you don’t do it first.”

He leaned his head back against the wall, like he could disappear into it.

Lis crouched in front of him — not threatening, not gentle. Her voice was soft. Almost affectionate. But it rang with something metallic beneath.

“Then make your last act useful,” she said. “Talk.”

Green swallowed hard. His throat worked like he was trying to choke down fear.

“I… I didn’t have a choice. I just did what they told me…”

“Where is my father?”

Her voice sliced through his like a blade — tight, trembling with tension. For a moment, her expression cracked. Grief, maybe. Or fury. But it vanished just as fast.

“I don’t know,” Green said, voice barely audible. “They said he was sent for correction, but… I wasn’t told where.”

His hands trembled. He clutched at his collar like a drowning man.

“I swear.”

The last word broke out of him — raw, desperate.

Lis studied him. And then, without a word, she drove her boot into his ribs.

The sound was dull, intimate. Like something important breaking.

Green gasped and folded in half, too shocked to scream. He didn’t even raise a hand to defend himself. Just curled up, trembling.

Royce didn’t flinch. He gave the smallest of nods — not approval, not condemnation. Just inevitability.

Green slowly uncurled. His face contorted, not just with pain — with shame.

“I… deserved that.”

Max, still standing in the background, felt something twist in him.

He wasn’t prepared for this — for Lis’s cold precision, her surgical violence.

He stepped forward, switched on the projector. The screen lit up inches from Green’s face.

A grainy video flickered to life: a man in a tailored suit, face pale and sharp. His voice was silent, but his lips moved with purpose, issuing orders through a headset.

The image was just clear enough to make out the features.

“Do you recognize him?” Max asked. His tone was flat. Not accusing — just absolute.

Green froze. His eyes widened. And in that moment, Max saw a fear that couldn’t be faked.

“Friedrich Steiner…” Green’s voice collapsed into a whisper. “Oh god.”

Max narrowed his gaze. He didn’t know the name — but the reaction told him everything.

“Who is he?”

Green’s mouth opened. Closed. Then finally:

“A monster. One of their top field operatives. The kind who smiles while he kills. The kind even the board’s afraid of. But the worst part…”

His voice dropped to a whisper.

“He reports directly to Iris.”

The name hit the room like a bullet.

Max didn’t speak. He didn’t have to.

Because suddenly, all the threads pulled taut. All the warnings, the shadows, the rumors — they all led to her.

Iris.

The woman with the frozen face. The one people didn’t dare name.

And now, her name was in Max’s path.

There would be no going around it. No going back.
